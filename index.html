A Starting Point In our introductory semester of my undergraduate program we were started with learning about what design is and came in touch with it visually. Interactive Media Design is a project based program, though the first semester was one of smaller scale. The given topic for this semester was to design a food truck service. My group created a tourist attraction that made it possible for tourists to experience the city both in a way of sightseeing and also in a way that allowed them to taste culinaric soul of the city. Out of that service we should then fashion a website and a prototpe for an interface our guests inside the truck. Because we distributed the work so that I was responsible for the creation of the website in HTML and CSS I will be focusing on this part of the project. If I had to choose now, my interest in developing the interface would be a lot greater than creating another website.

Game Programming For our subject Creative Coding I developed a game. The available options were classics like Pong, Snake or a self-designed game. My professor advised that self-designed games suited students more advanced so I was keen on giving it a try. Based on the animated TV-show Rick and Morty I created a browser bassed platforming game starring the show's two main characters with common and unique abilities. I also added items and enemies. You can check out and play the result here. While we only covered the basics of HTML, CSS and JavaScript (variables, if-statements, loops and arrays) I digged into object orientated programming and designed my code accordingly. I therefore learned approximately half of the second semester of coding within the first one. Since it's caused good resonance from both common students and my professor I was keen on diving into coding.

Experimental Design was the topic for our second semester. Based on that we should develop a simulation. We decided to make a piece of media that shows users how their actions influences their environment. Since climate changes is a hot topic we saw great relevance in it. We used the opportunity of experimental design to take a gamificated approach. With the right combination of storytelling and gamification we created a story of a traveling family. The user can take various actions that cause different outcomes at the end of the simulation. We very subtly tried to bring in options of littering so that Once the simulation was played through the user saw a fast-forward to a few years later when the family came to the same travel destination where the environmental changes had become visible. We then showed tips on how to improve the users decision-making in terms of environmental awareness.

Machine Learning A former student from the MIT came to our campus to teach us how to create rich user experiences with applied machine learning. While I've had red about the principles of neural networks before I had never before been in touch with the actual developement of a working model. Unfortunately our professor could not supervise our projects that much when we were in the hands-on phase of this course. By reading articles and following tutorials I finally was able to create a neural network that allowed you to capture footage through your webcam and then transfer it into a specific artstyle in a live manner. This was also the first time I came in touch with Python and libraries which made it surprisingly hard for me to archieve my goals. Even though it was a tough way to the finish line I am still interested in learning more about artificial intelligence.
